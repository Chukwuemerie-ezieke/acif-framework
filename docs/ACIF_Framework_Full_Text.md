# AI-Generated Content Integrity Framework for Curriculum (ACIF)
## Version 1.0 | Harmony Digital Consults Ltd | 2026

---

## INTRODUCTION & RATIONALE

Artificial Intelligence tools are increasingly used by educators, curriculum developers, and content creators to generate lesson notes, scheme of work, assessments, explanatory texts, and instructional materials. While AI accelerates content creation, it introduces unique risks for classroom use: factual inaccuracies (hallucinations), age-inappropriate content, culturally insensitive outputs, misalignment with national curriculum standards, and data privacy concerns when student data is involved.

This framework — the AI-Generated Content Integrity Framework (ACIF) — defines the standards, processes, and verification pipelines that must be followed before any AI-generated curriculum content reaches students in Nigerian schools and educational institutions at all levels (K–12+).

ACIF is designed to serve three contexts:
1. Internal policy at The Incubators Secondary Academy, Ufuma, Anambra State
2. Product integration within Harmony Digital Consults Ltd platforms (EduTrack Nigeria, Harmony School Suite)
3. Adoptable standard for any Nigerian or African school using AI in curriculum delivery

## SECTION 1: SCOPE AND DEFINITIONS

### 1.1 Scope
This framework applies to:
- All AI-generated or AI-assisted lesson plans, notes, worksheets, assessments, and study aids
- All grade levels: Early Childhood (ages 3–5), Primary 1–6 (ages 6–11), Junior Secondary JSS1–3 (ages 12–14), Senior Secondary SS1–3 (ages 15–17), and Pre-Tertiary/Bridge programs (ages 18+)
- All subjects aligned with the NERDC National Curriculum
- All AI generation tools used by staff including large language models (LLMs), AI writing assistants, image generators, and AI tutoring systems
- All digital and print content derived from AI outputs

### 1.2 Key Definitions
- **AI-Generated Content (AGC):** Any text, image, audio, or multimedia produced by an artificial intelligence system in whole or in part
- **Hallucination:** A confident but factually incorrect, fabricated, or misleading statement produced by an AI model
- **Age-Appropriateness:** Content that matches the cognitive, emotional, social, and developmental stage of the target age group
- **Factual Verification:** The process of checking AI-generated factual claims against authoritative, primary, or peer-reviewed sources
- **Content Integrity:** The state of curriculum content being accurate, age-appropriate, culturally safe, pedagogically aligned, and free from harmful or misleading information
- **Verification Gate:** A required checkpoint in the content pipeline where a specific standard must be met before content progresses

## SECTION 2: CORE PRINCIPLES

ACIF is built on eight core principles derived from UNESCO's Generative AI in Education Guidance (2023), ISO/IEC 42001:2023 (AI Management Systems), the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA), and NERDC curriculum standards.

**Principle 1: Human Agency and Oversight**
AI is a tool that supports human educators — never a replacement for teacher judgment. Every piece of AI-generated content must pass through a human review gate before student delivery.

**Principle 2: Factual Accuracy First**
Inaccurate information in the classroom causes lasting harm. All AI-generated factual claims must be verified against reliable sources. No unverified factual content reaches students.

**Principle 3: Developmental Appropriateness**
Curriculum content must match the cognitive, emotional, and social development of the target age group. Age bands, not just grade levels, govern content standards.

**Principle 4: Cultural and Contextual Relevance**
Content must be appropriate for the Nigerian socio-cultural context, respect religious and ethnic diversity, and align with national values as specified in the NERDC curriculum philosophy.

**Principle 5: Transparency and Documentation**
The AI source, generation prompt, verification steps taken, and human reviewers must all be documented for every piece of AI-generated content used in classrooms.

**Principle 6: Privacy and Data Protection**
Student personal data must not be used as input to AI generation tools without explicit NDPA-compliant consent. AI tools must be evaluated for data handling practices before institutional use.

**Principle 7: Continuous Improvement**
This framework is a living document. Verification methods, risk ratings, and age-band standards must be reviewed at minimum annually to keep pace with AI capability changes.

**Principle 8: Equity and Inclusion**
AI-generated content must not perpetuate bias, stereotyping, or discrimination based on gender, ethnicity, religion, or socioeconomic status. Bias screening is a required verification step.

## SECTION 3: CONTENT RISK CLASSIFICATION

All AI-generated curriculum content is assigned a Risk Tier before entering the verification pipeline. Risk Tier determines the number of required verification gates.

### Risk Tier Matrix

**TIER 1 — LOW RISK**
- Administrative and logistical content (timetables, activity schedules)
- Creativity prompts and open-ended discussion questions
- Template-based content (fill-in-the-blank worksheets)
- Required gates: Tier 1 Checklist + Educator Review
- Review time target: Same day

**TIER 2 — MEDIUM RISK**
- Lesson plans and scheme of work
- Explanatory notes for science, social studies, English, mathematics
- Story-based or narrative content for literacy
- Assessment questions (objective)
- Required gates: Tier 1 + Tier 2 Checklist + Subject Expert Review + Curriculum Alignment Check
- Review time target: 1–2 school days

**TIER 3 — HIGH RISK**
- Health and science content with factual claims (anatomy, reproduction, disease)
- History, government, and civic education content
- Religious and moral education content
- Essay-type and evaluation assessments
- Content mentioning real people, places, events, or statistics
- Any content for Early Childhood (ages 3–5)
- Required gates: Full 5-Gate Pipeline + Senior Educator Sign-off
- Review time target: 3–5 school days

**TIER 4 — CRITICAL RISK (RESTRICTED)**
- Content on sexuality, puberty, or sensitive health matters for minors
- Political or controversial social topics
- AI-generated content for students with special needs or trauma backgrounds
- Any AI content to be published externally or used for formal examinations
- Required gates: Full Pipeline + Institutional Director Approval + Legal/Compliance Review
- Review time target: 5–10 school days

## SECTION 4: AGE-APPROPRIATENESS FRAMEWORK

### 4.1 Age Band Classification

**BAND 1: Early Childhood (Ages 3–5, Nursery/Pre-Primary)**
- Maximum reading level: Pre-literacy (oral/visual content only)
- Vocabulary: Simple, concrete, single-syllable dominant
- Sentence length: Maximum 8 words
- Themes allowed: Friendship, family, animals, colours, shapes, basic numbers, nature, community helpers
- Themes prohibited: Conflict, death, illness, fantasy violence, any sexual or romantic reference, moral complexity
- AI image content: Must be cartoon-style only, no photorealistic depictions of people
- Review: All BAND 1 content requires specialist Early Childhood reviewer

**BAND 2: Lower Primary (Ages 6–8, Primary 1–3)**
- Reading level: Lexile 200–500
- Vocabulary: Familiar words with limited challenging vocabulary introduced in context
- Sentence length: Maximum 15 words
- Themes allowed: Expanded to include simple cause-and-effect, mild challenge and resolution, community, national identity
- Themes prohibited: Violence, explicit death descriptions, romantic or sexual content, fear-inducing scenarios
- AI content: Factual accuracy mandatory; cultural Nigerian context required where applicable

**BAND 3: Upper Primary (Ages 9–11, Primary 4–6)**
- Reading level: Lexile 500–800
- Vocabulary: Grade-level, subject-specific terms introduced with definitions
- Themes allowed: Multi-step narrative, national and civic themes, basic scientific concepts, global topics with Nigerian perspective
- Themes to handle with care: Death (natural, historical), simple discussions of illness, historical injustice
- Review: Subject teacher review required for all factual content

**BAND 4: Junior Secondary (Ages 12–14, JSS 1–3)**
- Reading level: Lexile 800–1100
- Vocabulary: Secondary-level academic vocabulary
- Themes allowed: Critical thinking topics, puberty (within Health Education framework), religious diversity, historical conflicts with balanced framing
- Special handling: Puberty and reproductive health content requires Senior Health Educator sign-off and parental communication protocol
- Review: Subject teacher + departmental review for Tier 2 and above

**BAND 5: Senior Secondary (Ages 15–17, SS 1–3)**
- Reading level: Lexile 1100–1400
- Vocabulary: Advanced academic and subject-specific terminology
- Themes allowed: Complex moral and ethical questions, current affairs, comparative religion, detailed biological sciences, economics, law
- Review: Subject Expert + Head of Department for Tier 3 and above

**BAND 6: Pre-Tertiary / Bridge (Ages 18+)**
- Reading level: University entry level
- All WAEC/NECO-aligned content must reflect official syllabus
- Critical thinking and independent analysis expected
- Content sourcing must be explicitly cited

### 4.2 Age-Appropriateness Screening Checklist

For each piece of AI-generated content, the reviewer must confirm:
- [ ] Target age band is clearly identified before AI generation
- [ ] Vocabulary complexity matches band specifications
- [ ] Themes do not include prohibited content for the band
- [ ] Cultural references are Nigerian or universally familiar (not geographically alien)
- [ ] Gender representation is balanced; no gender stereotyping
- [ ] Religious or ethnic references are respectful and balanced
- [ ] Visuals (if AI-generated) comply with age band visual guidelines
- [ ] Reading level assessed using Flesch-Kincaid or equivalent tool
- [ ] Content reviewed by an educator familiar with the specific age group

## SECTION 5: HALLUCINATION DETECTION PROTOCOL

AI hallucinations in educational content are especially dangerous because students and sometimes teachers lack the prior knowledge to identify them. This protocol provides a structured approach to detecting and eliminating hallucinations before content delivery.

### 5.1 Pre-Generation Controls (Prevention)

Before generating content, the content creator must apply these controls:

1. **Constrain the prompt**: Specify exact curriculum standards, grade level, subject, topic, and authoritative sources the AI should draw from. Example: "Generate a lesson note for JSS2 Basic Science on photosynthesis, following the NERDC curriculum. Only use established scientific facts. Do not fabricate examples."

2. **Use Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) where available**: Where AI tools support RAG (grounding responses in provided documents), supply authoritative source documents (NERDC curriculum texts, approved textbooks) as context.

3. **Request citations**: Always prompt the AI to cite sources for factual claims. Uncited factual claims are treated as unverified.

4. **Avoid high-uncertainty topics in AI prompts**: Topics with rapidly changing facts (current events, recent statistics) or highly specific local knowledge (local government data, specific Nigerian laws) must not rely primarily on AI generation.

5. **Generate multiple outputs**: For critical content, generate 2–3 AI outputs for the same prompt and compare for consistency. High variation between outputs signals low reliability.

### 5.2 Post-Generation Verification (Detection)

#### RED FLAGS — Automatic Tier Upgrade Required
The following in any AI output immediately escalate content to the next risk tier:
- Specific dates, statistics, or numerical claims without citation
- References to real living people in Nigeria or globally (risk of fabricated quotes)
- Claims prefaced by "According to [specific author/institution]" — must be verified
- Any mention of specific laws, regulations, or government policies
- Medical or health claims of any kind
- Historical events post-2020 (AI training cutoff risk)
- Claims that appear to contradict what the reviewer knows

#### VERIFICATION METHODS BY CONTENT TYPE

**Scientific and Mathematical Facts**
- Cross-reference with NERDC-approved textbooks
- Use multiple search queries across authoritative sources (WHO, NASA, NAN, government databases)
- Verify numerical values, formulas, and processes against at least 2 independent sources

**Historical and Civic Content**
- Cross-reference with Nigerian history textbooks and NERDC curriculum
- Verify dates and events through National Archives, established encyclopaedias
- Check for anachronisms, incorrect names of historical figures, or invented events

**Statistical Claims**
- Verify statistics against the source cited. If no source is cited, require the reviewer to find the original data
- Check National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) Nigeria for Nigerian data claims
- Check WHO, World Bank for global health/development statistics
- Flag all statistics older than 5 years as potentially outdated

**Biographical Information**
- Cross-reference biographical details with official sources (government websites, institutional biographies)
- Treat AI-generated biographical claims as UNVERIFIED until confirmed

### 5.3 The FACT Verification Method

Apply the FACT method to all Tier 2–4 content:

**F — Find the claim**: Identify every factual assertion in the content (not opinions, not questions)
**A — Assess the risk**: Is this claim checkable? Is it specific enough to be wrong?
**C — Cross-reference**: Verify against at least 2 independent authoritative sources
**T — Tag the result**: Mark each claim as VERIFIED ✓, MODIFIED (corrected), or REMOVED ✗

A content piece may not proceed to the next gate until all claims are tagged and no UNVERIFIED claims remain.

### 5.4 Hallucination Log

Every instance where hallucinated content is found and corrected must be logged in the institution's Hallucination Log, which records:
- Date of detection
- AI tool used
- Content type and subject
- Description of hallucination
- How it was detected
- Corrective action taken

This log informs periodic reviews of which AI tools and prompt types are most prone to hallucination.

## SECTION 6: THE FIVE-GATE VERIFICATION PIPELINE

All Tier 2, 3, and 4 content must pass through a structured Five-Gate Pipeline before reaching students. Tier 1 content requires Gates 1 and 5 only.

```
[AI Generation] → [Gate 1] → [Gate 2] → [Gate 3] → [Gate 4] → [Gate 5] → [Classroom]
```

### Gate 1: Pre-Delivery Screening (Automated + Creator Check)
**Who:** Content Creator (Teacher, Curriculum Developer)
**What to check:**
- [ ] Content is properly labelled as AI-generated
- [ ] Risk Tier assigned
- [ ] Age Band confirmed
- [ ] AI tool name and version recorded
- [ ] Generation prompt documented
- [ ] Red flags screened (see Section 5.2)
- [ ] Readability score checked (target Flesch-Kincaid grade level matches band)
**Pass criteria:** All items checked, no Red Flags present (or Tier upgraded if Red Flags found)

### Gate 2: Factual Verification (FACT Method)
**Who:** Subject Teacher or Content Reviewer
**What to check:**
- [ ] FACT method applied to all factual claims
- [ ] All claims tagged VERIFIED, MODIFIED, or REMOVED
- [ ] Zero UNVERIFIED claims remain
- [ ] Sources documented for verified claims
- [ ] Statistics checked against original source or flagged as unverifiable
**Pass criteria:** 100% of factual claims resolved; Hallucination Log updated if any hallucinations found

### Gate 3: Age-Appropriateness Review
**Who:** Educator with experience in the target age band
**What to check:**
- [ ] Vocabulary complexity matches band specifications
- [ ] Prohibited themes absent
- [ ] Cultural sensitivity verified
- [ ] Gender and ethnic representation checked for balance
- [ ] Reading level confirmed
- [ ] Sensitive topics (health, religion, social issues) handled per band guidelines
**Pass criteria:** All age-band criteria met; no prohibited themes present

### Gate 4: Curriculum Alignment Check
**Who:** Head of Department or Curriculum Lead
**What to check:**
- [ ] Content aligns with NERDC curriculum objectives for the specified level and subject
- [ ] Learning outcomes match curriculum expectations
- [ ] Assessment items (if present) match WAEC/NECO standard format where applicable
- [ ] Content does not introduce topics outside the curriculum scope for the level
- [ ] Pedagogical approach is appropriate (student-centred where required by NERDC)
**Pass criteria:** Confirmed alignment with NERDC curriculum; no scope violations

### Gate 5: Final Approval and Labelling
**Who:** Institutional Deputy Head (Academic) or Designated AI Content Officer
**What to check:**
- [ ] All previous gates have been completed and signed off
- [ ] Content is labelled with AI-source disclosure stamp
- [ ] Content is filed in the institution's AI Content Registry
- [ ] Any required parental communication (for sensitive topics) is prepared
- [ ] Content is saved in approved format (PDF, Word) for archival
**Pass criteria:** All gates complete; content formally approved and registered

## SECTION 7: DATA PRIVACY AND STUDENT PROTECTION

### 7.1 NDPA 2023 Compliance Requirements

All AI content creation and delivery activities involving student data must comply with the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023:

**Children's Data (Under 18):** Student data — including names, academic performance, and classroom responses — must NOT be entered into external AI platforms without:
- Written parental/guardian consent (for students under 18)
- Assessment that the AI platform has adequate data protection measures
- Documentation of the specific purpose and data minimisation approach

**AI Platform Assessment:** Before institutional use, each AI tool must be assessed for:
- Data retention and deletion policies
- Cross-border data transfer practices (NDPA restricts transfers outside Nigeria without adequacy determination)
- Whether the platform trains on user-submitted data
- Whether the platform is registered or recognised under NDPC guidelines

**Data Subject Rights:** Students and parents have the right to know what AI tools are used in generating content that affects their education. Schools must maintain a transparent AI Tool Registry that is accessible to parents/guardians on request.

### 7.2 Prohibited Data Inputs to AI Tools
The following data types must NEVER be submitted to AI generation tools:
- Student names or identification numbers
- Student academic records or grades
- Medical, disability, or special needs information
- Home addresses or family background information
- Photos or images of students (for AI image/analysis tools)

## SECTION 8: GOVERNANCE, ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES

### 8.1 AI Content Integrity Committee (ACIC)
Each institution implementing ACIF must establish an AI Content Integrity Committee comprising:
- **AI Content Officer (ACO):** Designated teacher or ICT coordinator responsible for overseeing pipeline compliance, maintaining logs, and conducting quarterly reviews. Chair of the ACIC.
- **Curriculum Lead:** Head of Department or Academic Director who manages curriculum alignment verification.
- **Data Protection Lead:** Responsible for NDPA compliance across all AI tool usage.
- **Parent/Community Representative:** Ensures community voice in AI content governance (for content transparency and communication).

### 8.2 Roles and Responsibilities Matrix

| Role | Generate AGC | Gate 1 | Gate 2 | Gate 3 | Gate 4 | Gate 5 | Log Hallucinations | Review Framework |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Class Teacher | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | — | — | — | ✓ | Input |
| Subject HOD | — | — | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | — | ✓ | ✓ |
| AI Content Officer | — | ✓ | — | — | — | ✓ | ✓ | Lead |
| Deputy Head (Academic) | — | — | — | — | ✓ | ✓ | — | Approve |
| Data Protection Lead | — | — | — | — | — | — | — | ✓ |

### 8.3 Escalation Procedure
If a verification gate cannot be resolved (e.g., a factual claim cannot be verified or disproved), escalate to the AI Content Officer. The ACO may:
- Reject the AI-generated segment and require human-authored replacement
- Seek external expert review
- Modify the content to remove the unresolvable claim
- Flag for framework review if the issue is systemic

## SECTION 10: IMPLEMENTATION GUIDE

### 10.1 For Schools Implementing ACIF for the First Time

**Month 1: Foundation**
- Establish the AI Content Integrity Committee (ACIC)
- Appoint AI Content Officer
- Complete AI Tool Inventory (list all AI tools currently used by staff)
- Conduct NDPA assessment of current AI tools in use
- Train all teaching staff on ACIF principles and Tier 1/2 gates

**Month 2: Pipeline Setup**
- Implement the Five-Gate Pipeline for all new AI-generated content
- Set up Hallucination Log (digital spreadsheet or this framework's companion tool)
- Set up AI Content Registry
- Conduct trial runs of pipeline with low-risk content

**Month 3: Full Operation**
- All AI-generated content entering the classroom passes through appropriate gates
- First quarterly review scheduled
- Parental communication prepared about AI tool usage and data protection

### 10.2 For Harmony Digital Consults Platforms

For schools using EduTrack Nigeria or Harmony School Suite, this framework is embedded into the AI Content Workflow module, which provides:
- Digital verification checklists for all five gates
